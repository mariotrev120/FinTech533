HW5: Keltner Squeeze Breakout, Expanding Walk-Forward, Exogenous ML, Hoeffding Regime Monitor

Three-pillar engine refit at every fold boundary. Long+short. Net of real trading costs and taxes. 2022-2025 out-of-sample across a survivorship-clean 50-name universe.

Historical backtest (no orders placed). Daily OHLCV bars pulled from IBKR TWS for 50 equities + 7 macro indices + 7 sector ETFs, all spanning 5 years (2021-01-04 to 2026-04-17). The strategy is refit at every fold boundary so no future data leaks into any fold’s training.

Engineering Philosophy

The assignment encourages keeping core logic simple. The base strategy here, Keltner Squeeze with vol+ADX confirmation, is intentionally that simple. The additional layers (exogenous LR, Hoeffding monitor, walk-forward refit, friction engine) are presented as labeled extensions on top of the base, not as the strategy itself. Each can be removed without affecting the breakout detector or trade simulation.

Strategy Logic

The strategy is the Keltner Squeeze Breakout: Bollinger Bands (20, 2 std) collapse inside Keltner Channels (20, 1.5 ATR) for at least one bar (the “squeeze on” regime of compressed volatility), then fire when the Bollinger Bands expand back outside the Keltner envelope. On the fire bar the close must also pierce the upper Keltner band (long) or the lower band (short). Two participation filters gate the trade: the fire-bar volume must be at least 1.3x its 20-day average, and ADX(14) on that bar must be >= 20 so the squeeze resolves into a real trend rather than chop. Exits: +4 × ATR profit target, -2 × ATR stop-loss, or 20 trading days timeout (market-on-close). Every trade pays IBKR-tiered commission, SEC and FINRA fees on sells, 5 bps per side of slippage, and a 3% annual short-borrow for shorts. The period’s aggregate net profit is taxed at the 30% short-term capital-gains rate.

Expanding Walk-Forward Discipline

The assignment literally says “rolling walk-forward window… helps demonstrate robustness and reduces overfitting.” Instead of a single frozen train/test split, the engine is refit at four fold boundaries:

Fold Training window OOS test year
1 2021 2022
2 2021-2022 2023
3 2021-2023 2024
4 2021-2024 2025

Training sets expand (never throw away history), so Fold 4’s LR is trained on every trade that was knowable by end-of-2024. At each fold: (1) BQS is recomputed from the training years, (2) StandardScaler is re-fit on training features only, (3) Logistic Regression is re-trained on training labels only. The strategy’s design parameters (BB/KC lookback, ATR multiples, exits, position sizing, cost model) and MIN_PROBABILITY = 0.50 are frozen across all folds , those are not learned from data, and retuning them per fold would re-introduce the overfitting the walk-forward is supposed to prevent.

Asset Selection: Survivorship-Clean Universe

Selection rule, committed ex-ante before any OOS result was viewed: “Top 50 US equities by Jan 1, 2021 dollar volume that traded continuously from Jan 1, 2020 through Apr 17, 2026, excluding ETFs and ADRs.” The rule is mechanical: a pre-dated liquidity ranking on a cutoff (Jan 1, 2021) that precedes the test windows, combined with a survivorship filter. The list blends 14 US-primary thematic names (AI/semis, nuclear, Bitcoin proxies, defense, GLP-1) with 36 large-cap US primary listings across financials, tech, healthcare, energy, consumer, payments, and media. ADRs are excluded by rule. Each name is scored by Breakout Quality Score per fold:

\[ \mathrm{BQS} = \left( \frac{n}{N} \right) \cdot (1 - f_{\text{false}}) \cdot \operatorname{med}(\Delta P_{10}) \]

where n is the confirmed squeeze breakouts in the training window, N is the number of training bars, f_false is the fraction of breakouts that close back inside the channel within three days, and med(dP_10) is the median 10-bar direction-adjusted return after the signal.

BQS drift across folds

Same names across columns = stable regime. Leaderboard shuffling = BQS is reactive to recent regime (disclose that in reading).

Breakout Definition

A confirmed squeeze-breakout signal fires on bar t when all five conditions hold:

  1. Squeeze on at t-1: Bollinger Bands lie fully inside Keltner Channels.
  2. Squeeze off at t: Bollinger Bands expand beyond Keltner (volatility released).
  3. Directional break at close(t): close pierces the upper KC (long) or lower KC (short).
  4. Volume confirmation: bar-t volume >= VOLUME_MULT = 1.3 × 20-day rolling average volume.
  5. Trend confirmation: ADX(14) >= ADX_MIN = 20 on bar t.

Reference bands use a t-1 shift (current bar never part of its own reference). Execution happens at the open of bar t+1 (no look-ahead).

Plain-English description of detect_breakouts

The function detect_breakouts(df) in breakout.py takes a daily OHLCV frame and returns a single series aligned to the input dates. On each bar it answers “did a confirmed Keltner-Squeeze breakout happen today?” with +1 (long), -1 (short), or 0 (no signal). Internally it builds the Bollinger Bands (SMA-20 ± 2 std) and Keltner Channels (EMA-20 ± 1.5 ATR), flags “squeeze on” bars where BB lies fully inside KC, and fires a signal when the squeeze transitions from on (t-1) to off (t) and the close of t pierces the appropriate Keltner band. It then adds the two participation confirmations (volume >= 1.3 × 20-day avg, ADX(14) >= 20). The t-1 index offset is the data-leakage guardrail: today’s close is compared against the Keltner and Bollinger levels computed on the previous 20 bars, not the last 20 bars inclusive of today, so today’s breakout is never part of its own reference set.

Exogenous ML Filter (Vestal’s Verbal Improvement)

Vestal’s critique: a model trained on a ticker’s own history is just learning its own noise. The filter therefore asks “is the macro environment conducive to momentum continuation today?” using strictly exogenous features (no ticker OHLCV enters the feature vector).

  • Yield curve spline coefficients (4) , degree-3 polynomial fit to {0.25y, 5y, 10y, 30y} Treasury yields (IBKR’s IRX/FVX/TNX/TYX indices). Level, slope, curvature.
  • VIX term structure (4) , VIX level, VIX3M level, VIX3M - VIX spread (contango or backwardation), 20-day VIX change.
  • IV - RV spread (3) , VIX (market IV proxy) minus 30-day realized volatility of SPY.
  • Broad-market regime (1) , 20-day SPY return.
  • Sector relative strength (2) , 5-day ticker return minus 5-day sector ETF return (relative measure only), sector 5-day return.

Per-fold refit. Every feature lookup uses the entry_date - 1 value. StandardScaler is fit on training-fold features only, then transformed on the test fold. Logistic Regression trained with class_weight='balanced', liblinear solver, C=1. Trained LR’s predict_proba attached to every trade for transparency.

Why LR is annotation, not a gate. Each fold’s LR trains on 10-30 labeled breakouts (the strict signal is that rare). A 0.50-threshold gate trained on that sample size would drop roughly half of an already underpowered OOS stream with high variance in which half gets dropped. Gating would make the pooled PnL more noisy, not less. The defensible role for this LR is diagnostic: coefficient-sign stability across folds tells you which exogenous feature directions generalize. A larger-N version of this study (more years or intraday bars) is where LR should be promoted to a gate or a sizing signal.

Hoeffding Regime Monitor (Egger & Vestal 2025)

Two one-sided monitors run on the concatenated OOS trade stream across all 4 folds (44 trades pooled). Under H0 (the pooled training-year regime persists), Hoeffding’s Inequality bounds the probability of observing an underperformance as large as the realized mu - X̄:

\[ P(\bar X - \mu \ge t \mid H_0) \le \exp\!\left(\frac{-2 t^2 N_{\text{eff}}}{(b - a)^2}\right) \]

N_eff = min(N × (1 - ρ)/(1 + ρ), N) corrects for trade-outcome autocorrelation. Alert thresholds: 50% (amber) / 25% (orange) / 10% (red → halt).

Cost Exposure

Every trade pays friction (commission + regulatory fees + slippage + short-borrow) and the aggregate positive net PnL is taxed. Gross → friction → tax → net.

Block-Bootstrap Confidence Intervals

A point estimate of net PnL on 44 OOS trades is a single-roll realization. A block bootstrap (block size 3, respecting local trade-outcome autocorrelation) resamples the pooled stream 10,000 times and reports the 95% CI on each metric - turning “point estimate” into “range consistent with the observed trade sequence”.

The observed pooled net PnL sits inside a 95% interval that spans both sides of zero: the data do not distinguish the strategy’s realized PnL from noise on this sample size.

Assumptions & Replicability

  • Data. IBKR TWS via shinybroker, 5 years of daily bars cached as parquet under FinTech533/Homeworks/HW5/data/. Each symbol covers 2021-01-04 through present. Ordinary trading hours only. Macro indices (VIX, VIX3M, TNX, FVX, IRX, TYX, SPY) and seven sector ETFs (XLK, XLV, XLU, XLF, ITA, URA, IBIT) are cached for exogenous feature construction.
  • Execution. Signals at close of bar t. Execution at open of bar t+1. If stop and target touch on the same bar, stop triggers first (conservative).
  • Slippage. 5 bps per side (10 bps round-trip), stressed to 10 and 15 bps.
  • Commission. IBKR Pro tiered at $0.0035/share, $0.35 min per order, 1% of notional cap.
  • Regulatory fees (sells). SEC Section 31 at 0.00278% of notional + FINRA TAF at $0.000166/share capped at $8.30.
  • Short-borrow. 3% annualized linear accrual on notional × hold_days / 252.
  • Taxes. Short-term capital gains at 30%, applied to period aggregate net positive PnL. Losses offset gains within the period. No carry-forwards modeled.
  • Position sizing. Fixed $10,000 notional per trade, whole shares only (rounded down), one open position per ticker at a time. Strategies run in parallel across 50 tickers.
  • ML. StandardScaler fit on each fold’s training features only. Logistic Regression with class_weight='balanced', solver='liblinear', C=1. Threshold at MIN_PROBABILITY = 0.50 is the Bayes-optimal decision under equal misclassification cost; it is frozen by design (not retuned per fold). LR probabilities are recorded on every trade as annotation; the equity curve and all artifacts reflect every strict-signal trade without LR gating. Features strictly exogenous (Vestal’s Law).

Strategy Diagnostics

The headline is a null (pooled net -$2,571 on 44 trades, 95% bootstrap CI straddles zero). The interesting questions are why the nominal edge didn’t materialize and why only 28 of 50 names ever fired.

Realized reward-to-risk is 1.1:1, not the nominal 2:1

Exit structure is +4 × ATR profit target versus -2 × ATR stop, i.e. nominal 2:1. Realized across the 44 OOS trades:

Exit type n Avg return Avg net PnL Avg hold (days)
profit_target 10 +12.96% +$1,290 8.9
stop_loss 21 -8.04% -$797 7.3
timeout 13 +0.98% +$98 20.0

Winners average +$772, losers average -$690 - realized reward-to-risk collapses from nominal 2.0 to 1.12. With 43.2% win rate, a 1.12:1 realized structure is ≈ 0.43 × 1.12 - 0.57 × 1.00 = -0.09 units per trade. Negative expectancy, matching the observed net loss.

The mechanism is the timeout bucket. 30% of trades never reach either exit band within the 20-day clock and close near entry. Nominal 2:1 assumes every trade resolves at the bands; a third of the sample doesn’t, and those near-flat exits dilute the reward side while the risk side still pays stop distances on the 48% that stop out. The three natural responses - a dynamic (ATR-scaled) timeout, a time-in-trade exit that takes partial profits, or ATR-adaptive stops that widen when VIX expands so winners don’t wash out - are beyond the frozen-design parameter set of this study.

28/50 firing rate reflects the 5-condition intersection

Every trade requires (1) BB inside KC the prior bar, (2) BB expands outside KC on the fire bar, (3) close pierces the corresponding KC band, (4) volume ≥ 1.3× its 20-day average, and (5) ADX(14) ≥ 20. The joint probability of all five is low by design. Five-year trade counts concentrate in higher-vol names (LEU 3, JPM 3, ABBV 3; RIOT, MARA, MSFT, META each 2); mega-caps with smooth trending or steady consolidation structures (AAPL, AMZN, MSFT-size, V, MA, KO, WMT) rarely generate the compressed-then-released BB/KC geometry that the rule requires. 22 of 50 names never fired a qualified signal in the full 5-year window. That is consistent with the strategy being a selection filter for vol-regime transitions rather than a broad equity signal - a design consequence, not an implementation gap.

Discussion

Pooled OOS result: 44 trades, 43.2% win rate, net -$2,571. Fold 1 (2022) -$5,116 on 6 trades, Fold 2 (2023) +$8,133 on 16 trades, Fold 3 (2024) -$3,802 on 16 trades, Fold 4 (2025) -$1,786 on 6 trades. The block-bootstrap 95% CI on pooled PnL spans roughly ±$11k and brackets zero; the win-rate CI (29.5% to 59.1%) brackets a coin flip. On this sample size, the strategy’s realized PnL is statistically indistinguishable from random. The headline is a rigorously-framed null, not a positive result, and the correct read is that 44 trades pooled across four walk-forward folds cannot separate a real edge from noise at any reasonable confidence level.

One fold (2023) paid positively. That is one observation, not a regime claim - Fold 2 is the single out-performer in a four-fold test, which is exactly the distribution a null would also produce. Reading any macro narrative into it requires more folds or more tickers, not more prose. What the per-fold numbers do support is the structural claim from the diagnostic above: winners that reach the profit target are consistent with nominal R:R (+12.96% avg at +4 ATR), but the 30% timeout bucket and 48% stop rate together compress realized reward-to-risk to 1.12:1 - that is the mechanical reason the pooled result is negative, and it is independent of the specific test years.

LR diagnostic value. With 10-30 training labels per fold, individual LR coefficients are not measurements. Sign stability across folds is the defensible read; magnitudes should be treated as noise. The BQS drift table plays the same role for ticker selection: stable top names = persistent breakout regime; churn = BQS is reactive to recent vol.

Where this would be strengthened. (i) Longer history with a pre-declared liquidity cutoff in an earlier year to stress regime stationarity across COVID and 2018; (ii) intraday bars to multiply trade-years by 6-16× and tighten Hoeffding; (iii) ATR-adaptive or dynamic exits to close the timeout-bucket leak without re-tuning the base signal; (iv) promoting LR from annotation to a sizing signal (not a hard gate) once the training set is large enough for a stable threshold.
